02 · the product

Three acts. Zero paperwork.

01

Watch

Owners subscribe to free expiration alerts. RDAP sweeps every 6h. Emails at 30 / 14 / 7 / 1 days. We also proactively score ~360K drops/day and pre-catch the ones people are likely to need rescued.

02

Catch

The moment a watched domain drops — or a high-scored candidate hits expiry — we fire a Dynadot backorder. Catch cost: ~$10. We hold it in custody for 30 days and point the DNS at our rescue page.

03

Rescue

Panicked owner visits their dead site → lands on a calm page that says "you haven't been hacked" → agentic chatbot walks them through payment → Dynadot transfer-out. 24 hours. Done.

08 · ethics wall

Guardrails in the business model.

This product only works if the founder can sleep at night. We baked the guardrails into the economics, not just the marketing.

What we don't do

  • Auction rescued domains to the highest bidder
  • Raise the price as the claim window closes
  • Upsell panic (concierge, priority queue, VIP)
  • Hold domains past the 30-day claim window
  • Email-spam the owner with urgency
  • Refuse hardship cases on principle

What we do instead

  • Flat $299. Same price for everyone.
  • Price locked on day one and displayed on page one
  • One tier. One fee. Transfer in 24 hours.
  • After day 30: donate to matching nonprofit, release, or sell at cost to original owner only
  • One rescue email + one reminder. That's it.
  • Reduced-fee program for nonprofits, community orgs, and hardship cases

Transfer-back guarantee + public incident log.

Every rescue ships with a 24-hour full-refund if the transfer fails on our end — no dispute forms, no phone trees. Every rescue is logged with domain, price paid, and transfer duration. If we ever drift from the wall, it's visible.
